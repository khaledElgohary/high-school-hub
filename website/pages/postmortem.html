Postmortem

A postmortem of the development experience.

What went right in the development process?

Notably, communication and teamwork were consistently strong throughout the development process. Before starting development for each iteration, we held at least one meeting to plan our tasks, assign work to team members, and resolve any doubts or problems from the previous iteration. This allowed us to start off each iteration on the right foot, ensuring everyone was on the same page and knew what they needed to complete for the iteration. Additionally, all team members regularly chatted with other team members to address issues, ask and answer questions, and discuss any matters they had on their mind. The frequent communication created an environment where we could work cohesively as a team in order to develop a better application.

Along with good communication, everyone played a role in developing a functional app. All team members were dedicated to the project and made important contributions to various aspects such as planning, development, and testing. Because of this, we were able to have a fully functional and stable app by the deadline for iteration 1 and 2, which was also a result of being able to work well together.

What went wrong in the development process?

While we completed all tasks early in iteration 1, we were pressed for time near the end of iteration 2, leaving us little time to test the application. Some of the work for the iteration was completed fairly late, and this impacted our ability to thoroughly verify that the new functionalities were working correctly. We have planned to start and complete our development work earlier for iteration 3, which should increase the time we have for polishing and improving the app as well as lessen the pressure of getting everything complete right before the deadline.

We also ran into some problems with merge conflicts and resolving them efficiently. Pulling changes from main into local branches was infrequent at times, which caused issues with attempting to merge these local branches into main later on. For iteration 3, we aimed to be more proactive in keeping our local branches up to date, making the process of merging back into main far smoother and less prone to mistakes when resolving potential conflicts.

How did the project change from our initial vision or stories? Did it work out as predicted?

We made a couple of reductions to the planned features for our app. For example, we initially wanted to implement admin users in our application. These admin users would be able to manage the high schools available for regular users of the application and create additional admin users to perform this task. However, we realized this may be excessive for the current scope of our app—we are currently only including high schools in Winnipeg, which greatly simplifies and decreases the high schools we can have available. Adding admin users at this time may be an unnecessary addition, though it is something we could theoretically consider implementing in the future.

Some of our features also grew bigger or more complicated than expected, and we sometimes moved these features into later iterations so we could have the time we needed to complete them. We also reduced the features that would make it into the app by the end of iteration 3 to account for this—some lower priority features have been cut from our initial plan.

Aside from the points above, the project does not differ much from our initial vision and stories; we have implemented the general functionality described in our vision statement.
